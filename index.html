LANDUSE AND LANDCOVER ANALYSIS
Land Use and Land Cover (LULC) Dynamics of Basirhat Block 1 (1993–2023)

This series of maps illustrates the temporal changes in Land Use and Land Cover (LULC) for Basirhat Block 1, spanning the years 1993, 2003, 2013, and 2023. The LULC classes include diverse land categories such as fish farms, rivers, vegetation, agriculture, urban areas, dry fallow lands, water bodies, and homestays with plantations (H.S.P.).

Key Insights:

Urbanization: A significant increase in urban areas (marked in red) is observed over the years, indicating rapid urban expansion.
Agricultural Decline: Agricultural lands have gradually decreased, likely due to urban encroachment and other land use changes.
Vegetation and Water Bodies: The reduction in vegetation cover and potential changes in water body extents highlight the environmental impact of human activities.
Fish Farms: The expansion of fish farming reflects local economic activities adapting to changing land use demands.
These maps were generated using high-resolution geospatial datasets and advanced GIS techniques, providing valuable insights into the land transformation processes of this region over three decades. This analysis supports sustainable land use planning and environmental conservation efforts in Basirhat Block 1.

This map illustrates the dynamic land use mixing of Basirhat 1 number block over three decades: 1993, 2003, 2013, and 2023. Spatial Distribution: Detailed spatial patterns showcasing the mixing of residential, commercial, agricultural, and other land use types within the block.
Data Accuracy: Use of remote sensing data and GIS methodologies ensures precise mapping and reliable analysis of land use changes.
Analytical Techniques: Advanced GIS techniques were employed to analyze and present the land use mixing, providing insights into the area's development dynamics.
This map serves as a crucial tool for urban planners, policymakers, and researchers to understand the land use evolution in Basirhat 1 number block and to make informed decisions for sustainable development.
